2025 Review - June

My First Computer!

For a long while I've wanted to build my own PC. This started as an just a vague ambition, but I then went through a phase of watching a lot of youtube videos about building PC's (especially gaming machines). I've always slightly resisted, as I've been a fan of game's consoles, mainly as I work at a desk all day, and then I don't want to sit at a desk to relax too. However, what pushed me over the edge was my back-garden telescope started producing a huge amount of data (especially with the hyperstar), and I needed a machine to store and process all the data. Of course, I went very over the top... here's a picture:


Surprisingly, it all went very well, despite the over-engineering... The scariest part was disassembling the GPU to install the water blocks for the custom water cooling. Luckily, the GPU survived, and the whole system works great with some custom overclocks, undervolting, etc... I even managed to install a optical HDMI cable to get the signal from the PC to my TV so I can still play games from the sofa. (Yes I know water-cooling doesn't really have any gains over air-cooling, I just wanted to see if I could as part of the building experience)

2025 Review - February

Six Nations

OK, I struggled to think of what to write about for February, its usually a busy month as its when I'm undertaking my lecture course. 2025 Review - January

First Hyperstar Image

I've been holding off posting about astrophotography until I setup the blog, but its been so long I thought I should include something. In late 2024 I obtained aa 'hyperstar' attachment for my telescope (that I use in my backgarden). I'll post a more detailed blog about hyperstar in the future, but broadly it converts the system from a f/10 to a f/2 system, massively increasing the field-of-view. In January 2025 I released my first deep image with the hyperstar - The Heart Nebula (click on it for a better view).


The image was taken with some ultra-narrowband filters with Halpha in red (10 hours), OIII in blue (10 hours), and SII in yellow/orange (11 hours), plus some RGB for the stars (40 min). With this image I love zooming in and seeing all the intricate details, like the dust clouds, and the random planetary nebula.

April 2023

My First Colour Astro Image from my Garden!


After making some upgrades to my telescope, I've made my first colour image with my telescope! The image is of M81 a very nearby galaxy; it's an Sab class so has a fairly chunky bulge but nice spiral arms and dust lanes. I potentially overdid the colours, but as a first attempt from my suburban garden I'm pretty happy. I'm planning to start a blog to document this, keep an eye on this space.

ATLAS Data Release 2

My paper describing the images for the second Herschel-ATLAS data release has been accepted! This marks the full release of the H-ATLAS data, the largest Herschel survey of the extragalactic sky. The maps cover an area approximately 660 square degrees, in the wavelength range 100 to 500µm. The paper can be accessed here.

May 2017

HASHTAG is a Go!

I've received the brilliant news that HASHTAG, a new large program on the JCMT has been approved! With its ~275 hours the survey will observe the whole of Andromeda with SCUBA-2 at 450 and 850µm, as well CO(J=3-2) with HARP in selected regions. The project has a PI for each region, and I'm the UK PI.
